About Me

Hey, I'm Omar Faruk Badhon, an embedded hardware enthusiast who speaks fluent datasheets, thinks in circuits, and occasionally dreams in Altium layers.

I graduated with a B.Sc. in Energy Science and Engineering from KUET, where I discovered my obsession with power electronics, embedded systems, and everything in between. Since then, I've been designing and building real-world hardware: remote monitoring systems, AC energy meters, smart controllers.
Currently, I work as an Embedded Hardware Engineer at telecom industry, where I've led the design and development of multi-layer industrial PCBs for telecom, energy, and automation systems. From schematic capture to BOM optimization and lab testing, I handle full-cycle hardware design — often under tight timelines and high expectations. I've also worked closely with international vendors to reduce cost while maintaining performance.

Outside of my 9-to-5, I lead a freelancing team on Fiverr, turning coffee into hardware for global clients. I've worked with protocols like RS485, Modbus, and UART, and designed power supplies, protection circuits, and real-time control systems.
